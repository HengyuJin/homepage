Biography

Hengyu Jin is pursuing a Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence at Carnegie Mellon University. He completed a dual Bachelor of Science degree track in Computer Science and Technology at Southwest Jiaotong University and Computer Science at the University of Leeds. His training covers core computer science foundations, including algorithms, operating systems, networks, artificial intelligence, databases, data mining, and software engineering.

His recent research focuses on secure and efficient deployment of network functions and inference agents in heterogeneous edge environments. He has formulated optimization problems, designed deployment algorithms, and conducted large-scale simulation studies. His accepted work at ICCC 2025 received the Best Student Paper Award. His work on NFV-based security-aware small-scale inference agents deployment in edge networks has been published in Computer Networks, and another paper on image rotation correction via diffusion and consistency models was accepted by ICAID 2025.

In addition to research, Hengyu has completed software development and AI system projects involving Flask APIs, MySQL data integration, PyTorch experiments, React Native applications, Node.js microservices, TensorFlow-based risk prediction, and IoT sensor platforms.

Research & Project Experience

NFV Security-aware Small-Scale Inference Agents Deployment in Edge Networks

06/2025 - 12/2025

Independent Project; supervisors: Associate Professor Danyang Zheng and Professor Fei Teng

  • Formulated the NFV-enabled Cost-Security Balanced Deployment problem for heterogeneous edge environments.
  • Proposed the CS-ND algorithm with a Cost-Security Efficiency Index for dynamic Security Network Function selection.
  • Validated through 1,843,200 simulations; achieved 59.5% cost reduction versus Security-first and 50.1% improvement over Cost-first.
  • Submitted manuscript to Computer Networks (CCF-B), under review.

Asymmetric Protection-based S-SFC Deployment for LLM-Inference-enabled Networks

12/2024 - 06/2025

Independent Project; supervisor: Associate Professor Danyang Zheng

  • Proposed asymmetric protection for security-aware service function chains.
  • Formulated the S-SFCEP problem with fault-disjointness and SeLA constraints and proved NP-hardness.
  • Designed the NS-SD algorithm; NY-20 simulations showed up to 56% total resource cost savings.

Image Rotation Correction via Diffusion and Consistency Models

04/2024 - 05/2025

Project Leader, team of 5; supervisor: Associate Professor Ao Luo

  • Combined consistency models and discrete wavelet transforms for image rotation correction.
  • Built DDPM, DDIM, and Stable Diffusion experimental frameworks in PyTorch on a subset of ImageNet.
  • Achieved PSNR 21.6 and SSIM 0.67, outperforming traditional rotational cropping and optical flow prediction methods.
